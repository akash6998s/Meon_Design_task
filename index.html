How does Aadhaar eSign enhance digital transactions?


Aadhaar and Aadhaar-enabled services have the potential to revolutionize domestic technological consumption in India. The Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) reports that Aadhaar has over 100 crores (1 billion) users, and Aadhaar-based eSign transactions have covered about 3.3 crores (33 million) people in 2017 alone. The banking and non-banking financial sectors have been at the forefront of adopting Aadhaar-based transactions.

The widespread implementation of Aadhaar authentication necessitates the deployment of many biometric devices, which can stimulate the hardware industry in the country. Using an authentication method, alongside fingerprint recognition, is expected to drive the sale of iris-enabled mobile devices.


Aadhaar-related technologies are not limited to hardware but have implications for software applications. Aadhaar can streamline customer sign-up, employee onboarding, employment verification, and documentation formalities. Companies like Meon Technologies have developed Aadhaar-enabled eSign products that allow corporations to digitize documentation work and facilitate signing contracts and agreements.

Payment service providers are leveraging Aadhaar to offer Aadhaar-enabled payment systems (AEPS) and perform electronic Know Your Customer (eKYC) processes for their merchants. AEPS has been particularly useful in providing cash-in and cash-out services to customers in rural and semi-urban areas. However, implementing Aadhaar-based payment systems requires upgrading communication networks to accommodate the transmission of biometric data.


Startups and tech companies are also capitalizing on Aadhaar to provide user-friendly services. Meon Technologies, for example, has simplified the mutual fund investment process using Aadhaar eKYC. Previously, the KYC process involved printing, signing, and couriering documents, but now it can be completed online and paperless through Aadhaar eKYC.

The demand for Aadhaar-enabled services is growing rapidly, leading to a surge in the production of Aadhaar-enabled biometric systems and payment services. Market reports indicate a 200% increase in demand for Aadhaar-enabled payment systems in the last quarter and a more than 1500% surge in demand for Aadhaar card-making services.

Aadhaar has also facilitated instant account opening and verification. With the amendments to various regulations, opening limited functionality accounts for savings and loans through paperless eKYC using Aadhaar-linked mobile phones is now possible. This reduces the need to visit a branch or submit physical paperwork. This shift towards paperless access to financial products is just the beginning.


The data collected online suggests that Indian consumers prefer submitting documents through secure online platforms rather than offline processes involving sales agents and physical paperwork. This indicates that Indian consumers are comfortable transacting online using Aadhaar as the authentication mechanism.

The impact of Aadhaar linking and enabled services has led to increased demand for various technologies. Software companies, hardware manufacturers, and handset makers are experiencing growth in their India business. Dell EMC, for instance, recognizes a $26 billion market opportunity in India and aims to leverage the government's JAM (Jan Dhan-Aadhaar-Mobile) initiatives to strengthen its position in the Indian market.

Overall, Aadhaar and Aadhaar-enabled services can potentially drive significant changes in domestic technological consumption in India, benefiting both the hardware and software industries and transforming how people access financial services, conduct transactions, and engage with various sectors of the economy.
